Yes, many countries have restrictions on specific goods such as weapons, pharmaceuticals, chemicals, endangered species, and high-tech items. Always check the regulations of both the importing and exporting countries before trading.

Customs duties are taxes imposed on imported goods. They are typically calculated as a percentage of the goods' value and may vary depending on the product type, origin, and destination. Countries may have tariffs or preferential rates under free trade agreements.

Most shipping companies provide tracking services. You can track the shipment through the carrier's website using the tracking number provided when the shipment was dispatched.

Incoterms (International Commercial Terms) define the responsibilities of buyers and sellers for the delivery of goods under international sales contracts. They clarify who is responsible for shipping, insurance, import duties, and other logistics.

Tariffs are taxes imposed on imports and can increase the cost of foreign goods. They may affect the pricing of imported products or raw materials, which in turn can impact your profit margins.

You can search for freight forwarders and shipping companies through online directories, trade organizations, or recommendations from other businesses. Ensure the company you choose has experience with international trade and offers services for your specific needs.
